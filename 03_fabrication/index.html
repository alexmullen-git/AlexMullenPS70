<div class="textcontainer"> <div class="page-header"> <h1>Week 3: Hand Tools and Fabrication</h1> <p>Building a kinetic sculpture with motors and fabrication techniques</p> </div> <div class="assignment-card"> <h2 class="assignment-title">Kinetic Sculpture</h2> <div class="assignment-content"> I built a crawling robot that has two opposite-timed arms that move it forward. The robot is powered by a three-volt system that uses two AA batteries in series with a switch to turn it on and off. I designed two laser-cut friction fitting adapters for the motor shafts (picture below). Initially I had trouble getting the robot to actually move forward instead of flailing around. This called for two design changes: first I added hot glue pads on the legs to increase friction with the ground, and second I added a dowel with two bearings to stabilize the back of the project and reduce forward-moving friction. </div> <div class="image-grid"> <div class="image-wrapper"> <img src="./wide.jpg" alt="My kinetic sculpture"> </div> </div> <div class="image-grid"> <div class="image-wrapper"> <img src="./adapter.jpg" alt="adapter"> </div> <div class="image-wrapper"> <img src="./leg.jpg" alt="arms"> </div> <div class="image-wrapper"> <img src="./wheels.jpg" alt="wheels"> </div> </div> <div class="assignment-content"> There's also a video here! </div> <div class="video-wrapper"> <video controls> <source src="./vid.mp4" type="video/mp4"> </video> </div> </div> </div>
